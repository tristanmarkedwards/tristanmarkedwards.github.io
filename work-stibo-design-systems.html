Context

Scaling consistency across a growing platform

PXDC is a multi-product platform for onboarding, managing, enriching, and distributing product content at scale. As the platform evolved, multiple product teams began solving similar problems in different ways, creating inconsistency across experiences and increasing the cost of change.

My role spans product design and design-system strategy. Alongside owning Product Onboarding and Product Data Syndication, I help define and evolve the shared foundations used across PXDC, building on earlier work within Enhanced Content — a scalable content-management experience supporting retailers ranging from Amazon and Walmart to smaller regional partners.

Design challenge

Consistency across teams without slowing delivery

PXDC is developed by multiple teams working on different customer problems in parallel. Over time, this led to a growing number of custom components, inconsistent page structures, and diverging interaction patterns across products.

The challenge wasn't just visual consistency. It was creating enough shared structure that teams could build faster and more coherently, without introducing a system that became a bottleneck for delivery.

Focus areas

How the system evolves through product work

The design system at PXDC doesn’t exist separately from product work — it evolves through it. Most of the system development happens while designing and shipping real features across onboarding, syndication, and content workflows.

01

Identifying patterns through delivery

While designing product features, I identify repeated UI patterns, inconsistent interactions, and duplicated solutions across teams that can be consolidated into shared foundations.

02

Consolidating into shared system elements

These patterns are refined into reusable components, interaction models, and guidelines that can be adopted across multiple product teams.

Approach

Improving systems through alignment, not control

Design-system work at this scale is less about producing components and more about aligning teams around shared ways of working. The goal is to enable consistency without requiring central approval for every decision.

I work closely with designers and engineers to identify duplication, consolidate patterns where appropriate, validate implementation quality, and evolve the system through real product delivery. More recently, I’ve explored AI-assisted workflows to reduce repetitive system maintenance while preserving consistency.

System tension

Diverse users, shared foundations

PXDC supports a wide range of users across the product-data lifecycle — from onboarding teams and content specialists to organisations managing large-scale syndication workflows across multiple channels.

These users have different goals, workflows, and levels of expertise, but rely on the same underlying platform. The challenge is creating experiences that support specialist needs without fragmenting the overall system.

Impact

Creating a more scalable platform

01

Reduced duplication

Consolidated patterns and interaction models that had previously evolved independently across product teams.

02

Improved consistency

Strengthened coherence across onboarding, syndication, and content-management experiences.

03

Better cross-team alignment

Helped teams converge on shared patterns, improving collaboration between design and engineering.
