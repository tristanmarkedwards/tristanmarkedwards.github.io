Overview

One platform, several product teams, one system

Stibo Systems builds enterprise platforms for managing and distributing product data and content at scale. Within PXDC, I own design for two product areas — Product Onboarding and Product Data Syndication — while also driving design-system strategy across the wider suite, including the foundations I helped lay during Enhanced Content, a scalable below-the-fold content experience for retailer product pages such as Amazon.

The challenge

Multiple teams shipping in parallel, without a shared language

Enterprise platforms like PXDC are built by several product teams moving at once. Without a shared system of patterns, components, and vocabulary, a platform fragments quietly — duplicated effort, inconsistent interactions between modules, and a steeper learning curve for customers who move between them. The job isn't just designing good individual screens; it's making sure good decisions made in one product don't have to be re-made from scratch in another.

Focus areas

Where the design-system work actually lives

01

Cross-product design system

Reusable interaction patterns and a shared component vocabulary, so individual product teams can build consistently without needing to coordinate on every single screen.

02

Specific products & features

Product instances that utilise the centralised design information of the cross-product design system.

Approach

Consistency without slowing teams down

The hard part of design-system work at this scale isn't drawing the components — it's keeping platform-wide consistency from becoming a bottleneck for any one product team. I work closely with engineering on implementation validation to make sure what ships matches intent, and I've been exploring where AI-assisted design workflows can remove repetitive work without compromising the consistency the system exists to protect.
